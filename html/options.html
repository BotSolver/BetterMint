Features



  • Depth: Determines the maximum depth to which the engine will search during its analysis. Increasing the depth allows for a more thorough analysis but requires more computation time.
  • Elo: Represents the engine's estimated playing strength in Elo rating points. Helps adjust the engine's performance and playing style.
  • Skill Level: Controls the playing strength of the engine. Affects move selection, evaluation, and search parameters. Higher values make the engine stronger, while lower values make it weaker.
  • Lines: Determines the number of principal variations the engine will output during its analysis. Provides additional lines of analysis, showing the engine's top moves and evaluations.
  • Threads: Specifies the number of CPU threads the engine can utilize for analysis. More threads speed up the search and analysis by parallelizing the computation.
  • Limit Strength: Restricts the engine's playing strength to a specific level. Useful for setting an upper limit on the engine's strength regardless of its actual capabilities.
  • NNUE: Stands for "Efficiently Updatable Neural Network." Refers to a type of neural network used in Stockfish for position evaluation. Provides more accurate assessments of positions.
  • Auto Move Time: Uses math from all the delay settings to delay the auto move fire time.
  • Mate Finder: Looks for the fastest checkmate from the current position.
  • Random Best Move: Makes the auto move choose a random best move from its list, adding randomness to make it appear more natural.
  • Legit Auto Move: Automatically moves the pieces for you while emulating a legitimate player, introducing mistakes and inaccuracies along the way.
  • Best Move Chance: Enables you to control the accuracy of the engine's move selection by adjusting the chances assigned to each move. Higher chances increase the probability of the engine playing what is considered the best move, allowing for more precise decision-making. Lower chances, on the other hand, can introduce randomness or alternative strategies. By fine-tuning these chances, you have greater control over the engine's move selection and overall accuracy, ensuring it aligns with your analysis or desired gameplay approach.
  • Show Hints: Displays the moves you can make from best to worst based on the engine's depth of analysis.
  • Text-to-Speech: Converts the moves into spoken words, allowing you to act legitimately while streaming. For example, it can say "a2 a4". Please note that moves are in uci format (e.g. e2e4 means pawn to e4 in starting position)
  • Move Analysis: Provides feedback on the quality of your moves or the opponent's moves.
  • Depth Bar: Shows the progress of the engine's analysis and indicates when it's done.
  • Evaluation Bar: Displays an evaluation of the current position in the game, indicating whether you are in a winning position or not.

Each setting plays a role in adjusting various aspects of the engine's behavior, such as search depth, playing strength, move selection, evaluation accuracy, and more. These settings can be customized to fine-tune the engine's performance and adapt it to specific requirements or preferences.
